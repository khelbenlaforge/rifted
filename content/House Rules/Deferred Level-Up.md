# Deferred Level-Up

Borrowed from Brennan Lee Mulligan's approach to campaign pacing.

## The Rule

When the DM announces that the party can level up, they do **not** all level up immediately. Instead, each player may choose to trigger their level-up at **any point from that moment forward** — including mid-combat.

## How It Works

- The DM signals: *"You can all start leveling up to Level X."*
- Each player decides independently when to claim that level.
- Leveling up mid-combat is legal. If a character would benefit from the extra hit points, class features, or proficiencies in the moment — they can use the level-up right then.
- **Mid-combat HP:** Leveling up grants the rolled HP increase but does **not** heal the character. They simply have more maximum hit points from that point forward.

## Why This Works

It treats leveling up as a **narrative moment** — an awakening of potential — rather than a bookkeeping event between sessions. It gives players agency over when their characters reach into their reserves. It can be dramatically satisfying: a character on the edge of death claiming their third level as they find something to fight for.

## First Used

Session 12 — party granted access to Level 3 via this mechanic. Most leveled up before the long rest.
