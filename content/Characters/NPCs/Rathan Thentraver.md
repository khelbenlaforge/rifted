## Appearance

Broad-shouldered, dark-haired with grey beginning at the temples. Round face. Laughs with his whole body. Always looks slightly like he just came from somewhere fun and is heading somewhere else fun. Wears his armor like he sleeps in it, which he occasionally does.

## Personality

He loves people — genuinely, uncynically. Reads rooms quickly, not for tactical reasons, just because he finds people interesting. Jovial, generous, fond of wine and gambling. Brave when it counts.

He is not suspicious by nature. This is what makes his unease about "Torm" so pointed — he can't name what's wrong. He just knows Torm is *off*, and that sits uncomfortably in someone who doesn't usually carry discomfort.

His faith in [[Tymora]] is experiential, not philosophical. Things work out for him. He has attributed this entirely to Lady Luck for so long that the attribution has become true.

## Background

A Knight of Myth Drannor and the longtime best friend of [[Torm of the Dales]] — Torm the halfling thief, not the god. Their friendship is the Knights' most storied partnership: drinking buddies, co-conspirators in bad ideas, and genuine anchors for each other. Rathan has followed Torm into worse situations than this one.

## Role in the Story

Already at [[Elminster]]'s Tower when the prequel party arrives (Event 3, Session 14). He was not at the Old Skull Inn during the courtyard encounter — but Shadowdale is a small town. By the time the party arrives, he has already heard about the beast.

First sight of "Torm" (JJ): relief, warmth, a bear hug if allowed. Then a half-second where something flickers in his eyes. At a quiet moment aside from the group: *"I heard what happened at the inn. Are you alright? You seem… quieter than usual."*

He will not push. He will watch. If JJ plays the friendship well, Rathan will be convinced — for now. If JJ misses a beat, Rathan covers for him anyway. That's what you do for your best friend. He just won't stop watching.
