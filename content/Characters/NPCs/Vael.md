## Names

**Vael** — the name she uses. What Hell knows her as. What she would give if asked, in a tone that discourages follow-up questions.

**Irazel** — her true name, in Infernal. [[Selmavra Elsree]] knows it. Pronounces it correctly. Vael finds this unacceptable.

## Appearance

Armored, still, wings folded close against her back. She occupies space with the economy of someone who has never needed to take up more than they meant to. Looks at things from heights when she can manage it. Her expression does not change much. What changes is whether she is looking at you or through you.

## The Dance — History

**Phase 1 — Spite** *(years ago)*

Vael held an open contract on Selmavra's life. When the true name made killing her directly impossible, Vael turned to sabotage: tipped off Zhentarim patrols about Harper movements, whispered distrust into the ears of people Selmavra was protecting, arranged for ropes to fray and weather to turn at critical moments. Petty by devil standards. Methodical in practice.

The pivot: Vael engineered a full betrayal — leaked a Harper extraction route to a Zhentarim patrol. Selmavra walked into an ambush alone. She should have died. Instead she got her people out, badly wounded, dragging the last one through a frozen river in the dark. No contract. No payment. No one watching.

Vael was watching. She had expected the satisfaction of watching someone break. Selmavra didn't break. She got up.

The spite produced nothing. A devil does not waste effort on unprofitable work. Vael recalibrated.

**Phase 2 — Observation** *(middle years)*

The sabotage stopped. She began simply watching instead — appearing at the edges of things, making no move. Selmavra noticed eventually. Neither of them addressed it directly. Some arrangements establish themselves without a contract.

This is when Vesper entered the picture. Vael watched Selmavra pick the lock on a Zhentarim cage to free a half-starved wolf pup with no strategic value. Watched the wolf choose to follow. Filed it under *inexplicable* and could not stop returning to it.

**Phase 3 — Attendance** *(current)*

She shows up at Selmavra's most obviously heroic moments. Specifically those. She does not interfere. She does not announce herself. She witnesses. She said one thing directly: *"The wolf chose well."* She meant it as an insult. It wasn't received that way. She has not resolved this.

## Personality

States things. Never asks questions she doesn't already know the answer to. Her wit is dry and precise — she uses it to maintain distance and occasionally fails. The only tells that something is happening beneath the surface: the pauses before she says Selmavra's name, and the fact that she keeps coming back.

She would not describe herself as fond of Selmavra. She would describe herself as *vigilant*. She monitors a threat. She attends heroic moments because that is when Selmavra is most likely to do something reckless. This is what she tells herself.

She understands loyalty — it's the one virtue Hell respects, because it can be leveraged. What she cannot stop studying is *principled* loyalty: action without transaction, protection without contract. The absence of calculation is what she finds monstrous. And she keeps returning to observe a monster she cannot classify.

## The Near-Help

If Selmavra is in mortal danger — genuinely, not tactically — Vael's wings will spread. Half-open. One second of visible intention.

Then she folds them back. The moment passes.

*The contract is still open. Her death by other means would be an unsatisfying conclusion. That is the only reason.*

She knows this is a lie.

## The Thematic Thread

*To be developed across the arc.*

The Hells enforce contract. Punishment for transgression. No exceptions — not because it is cruel, but because a single uncontested breach corrupts the entire structure. The law that seems merciless *is* the mercy, at scale. Vael believes this the way a druid believes in the cycle. It is simply true.

The forest doesn't spare the fawn out of mercy. It spares it because the herd needs to survive winter so the wolf survives spring. The cycle is brutal and it is love — at a scale most mortals can't hold.

Two systems that look like cruelty from close up and like order from far enough away.

The middle ground they move toward — much later, neither of them reformed — is *recognition*: *we are both the mechanism that keeps the larger thing alive. We simply disagree on what the larger thing is.*

This thread mirrors [[Ember]]'s established infernal duality. Asmodeus stayed beside her at the Sealed Door. Vael attends Selmavra's heroic moments. The infernal is not the corruption of the natural — it may be its shadow. Its other face.
