## The Vision Begins

It starts with a hand.

[[Augustus Kennedy]] reaches out in the Sanctum — because he always does — and touches the mural. The cosmological figures carved into that ancient stone surge with light, and something vast and old and hungry for witnesses reaches back. In an instant, the five of them are somewhere else in their minds. Three in body.

Only [[Jonathan Jordan]], [[Gill Bates]], and [[Lea]] are present at the table tonight. They watch their own pasts.

---

## Lives They Left Behind

The vision doesn't begin in a dungeon or a dying world. It begins in the ordinary.

**Jonathan Jordan** sits across from his agent in Los Angeles. The pitch is good. The script is good. The agent's response is the same as always: *you're not a name yet.* The other line ringing is his part-time employer asking where he is — the pool won't clean itself. JJ has a gift for story. The world keeps offering him buckets.

**Gill Bates** sits at the family table in Greece. His parents want him to call his cousin in Athens, the one who is doing so well as a businessman — the same cousin who, when they were children, salted his fish. Gill wants none of it. He wants the boats. He wants the coast and the catch and the sea. His cousin would understand nothing of that, and Gill trusts nothing he cannot see with his own hands.

**Lea** descends into the living room of a Singapore mansion — the kind of wealth that a foreigner sees from the outside and cannot quite believe is real. Her mother is seated. There is talk of a matchmaking appointment tomorrow: a foundation chairman's son, very suitable. Lea is resistant, but her mother has a softer edge to her pressure tonight. She suggests postponing — and perhaps a visit to the private doctor first, for some Botox. *You don't need to look thirty-six, darling.*

*I am thirty-six,* Lea replies. *I should look thirty-six.*

Her mother coughs. The subject is tabled. The subject is never truly tabled.

The three scenes play out like memories set in amber. But the three people watching them are not *in* these moments — they are outside them, watching themselves from a distance. The recognition arrives slowly: these events took place *after* they were already gone from Earth. This is what their world did without them. The gap they left, lived in by echoes.

Then the scenes dissolve.

---

## The Night of the Storm

They land in a tavern in Arabel, Cormyr. Not in their own bodies.

[[Lea]] is [[Midnight]] — a raven-haired wizard of some reputation, carrying a blue-white star pendant seared into the skin of her collarbone. She met [[Mystra]] the night of the Storm and received her trust. The pendant grants her *pass without trace* at will, and one chosen spell — she has chosen *magic missile* — that she may cast four times without consuming a slot.

[[Jonathan Jordan]] is [[Torm of the Dales]] — a Knight of Myth Drannor, a Harper working undercover in Arabel. He knows things about this city and certain people within it that he has not yet disclosed to anyone in this room.

[[Gill Bates]] is [[Adon]] — a cleric of [[Sune]], devoted, good-hearted, and openly vain. He cares about his image in a way that is not cruel but is very, very consistent.

The others in their company are [[Cyric]], a rogue of uncertain allegiance who moves like he is always measuring exits, and [[Kelemvor]], a fighter who charges fairly for his work and will never explain why.

Outside, the Storm is breaking. This is no ordinary weather — the world's sky is coming apart, and within it, the gods of the Forgotten Realms are falling like stones. They have been cast out of their divine planes by [[AO]], who found the Tablets of Fate stolen from his vault. Until those tablets are returned, the divine are walking among the mortal, lessened, angry, and dangerous. Magic is unreliable now. Every spell cast rolls against a chaos table: sometimes nothing, sometimes too much, sometimes something else entirely.

The party settles. They drink. They find each other.

---

## Caitlan's Plea

Some days pass. A girl appears.

[[Caitlan Moonsong]] — young, blond, barefoot, wearing dark robes — walks into the tavern and asks for adventurers. She has walked far to find people who might help her, she says. Her mistress has been taken captive. She cannot pay them, but the reward will be great once her mistress is free. She offers her word, and the word of her mistress.

She will not say who her mistress is.

The party agrees. Caitlan arranges to meet them to lead them out of the city, then slips away — and a woman nearby mentions that the girl left looking pale, blood down her side, with a cleric of Lathander or Ilmater.

That night, the party dreams of chained wrists in darkness and cruel laughter.

When they gather to leave, Caitlan appears — ghastly white, fevered, purple veins at her chin and arms. She cannot come with them. *Castle Kilgrave,* she says. *A tower. My mistress. Please go. I will follow when I can.*

She collapses. A priest of Ilmater tells them: *go, and she will recover. If not — his voice trails away.*

They go.

---

## Castle Kilgrave

The castle wears its malice openly. The courtyard is fog-thick and blood-smelling, and within a few steps the party is separated — each of them sent into a private hallucination drawn from what they want most.

The mist shows them their desires. Some resist. Some do not. In the end, all of them make it to the grand entry hall, and the freed members work to pull the others back through the illusions. Cyric is helpful in ways no one anticipated. Caitlan eventually tumbles through a side door — she made it, somehow, bloody and resolved — and points at an electrum door that was not visible before.

On the second-floor landing, the party finds a magic circle inscribed into the stone. It is a complex working, layered. Through arcana and investigation checks, they determine: one layer controls the fog; another the hallucinations; a third and fourth control the seal and the magical trap on the door at the back of the hall.

The door bears a symbol of [[Bane]]. When [[Kelemvor]] and [[Torm of the Dales]] approached it, they were each given a saving throw — one of them takes necromantic damage. The trap is real.

They dismantle the circle layer by layer. The door opens.

---

## The Chamber of Chains

Beyond the door: red smoky mist. Within it, something struggles — a being of pure magical energy, elemental-grade, chained with eldritch bonds that do not look like they were made to hold a person. White skeletal hands, each animated and crawling, circle the perimeter.

The party fights. The shadow creatures come from the module's script but the chaos table adds texture — spells misfire, blossom, reverse, or land with maximum devastation at random intervals. They are victorious.

Caitlan steps forward. She moves with a certainty she did not have before. When she touches the struggling form in the mist, [[Mystra]] — the Goddess of Magic, her avatar captured and chained by [[Bane]] — fades from her bonds and occupies the girl. [[Bane]] departs. The castle's supernatural impositions dissolve, leaving only the ruins of an old fort.

[[Caitlan]], now [[Mystra]], approaches [[Midnight]]. She asks for the pendant back. [[Midnight]] returns it.

[[Mystra]] opens a gate: purple mists, shifting darkness, a staircase into the void.

---

## The Guardian at the Gate

They follow her through.

The void is vast and chiming. [[Mystra]] has grown — taller, lit from within, walking on nothing toward a golden light that resolves, as they draw closer, into a figure in full plate armor. Twenty feet tall. Visor down. The glow around him brightens from gold to white.

**[[Helm]].**

*Mystra. You come not alone. Have you the Tablets?*

She does not. She demands passage anyway. She is angry in the way of someone who has been diminished and cannot stop thinking about what was taken. Helm is gentle and implacable. He will not move. He was commanded not to let any deity pass without the Tablets, and he is the kind of god who follows orders because he believes in what the orders protect.

Mystra attacks. Everything she throws at Helm — fire, blades, tentacles, bolts, hammers of force — is absorbed or ignored. She weeps blue-white tears that burn, and she does not stop. At last she hurls herself at him. Their gazes meet. He draws back his gauntleted fist and strikes.

The body of Caitlan Moonsong explodes.

The party, shielded by Mystra's will, is left standing on a pedestal of stone — the only solid circle remaining in a landscape of charred waste. The castle, everything within half a mile, has been reduced to powder. The air has a particular smell.

[[Helm]] lowers his visor. When the party can speak again, he speaks to them.

---

## Helm's Message

*Those you know as gods now walk the Realms among you. The reasons for this are many, and better unsaid. Know this: they all — great and small — are now much lessened in power, and they all seek something that is missing.*

*Men did not hide them, but men — or elves, or dwarves, or orcs, or any creature of Faerûn — may find them. They are the Tablets of Fate. They have many names, many forms. But now they appear as stone tablets, as long as a man's arm on each side, inscribed with the names of all the gods and their duties — which so many of them seem to have forgotten.*

*Their recovery is crucial to the survival of the Realms as you know them.*

*If you are heroes, seek them. If not, tell those who are of what I say. Great glory awaits, in the seeking as well as the finding. Go now, in safety. Think, and grow wiser than the one you came with.*

The gate closes.

At [[Midnight]]'s feet: the pendant. It will not let anyone else touch it. When she picks it up, it shines, and she knows: she carries a shard of [[Mystra]]'s broken power. And she knows something else — that she must find [[Elminster]], in Shadowdale.

---

## The Road North

The party decides to go to Shadowdale. [[Torm of the Dales]] does not mention that he knows [[Elminster]].

They travel. Days pass. At a night camp, [[Kelemvor]] slips away from the fire, alone.

He does not return.

[[Torm]] notices. [[Midnight]] notices. They find him in the dark — unconscious on the ground, blood on his clothes and the grass around him. A deer has been killed nearby. Torn apart, not butchered. There is black fur caught in the underbrush, wrong for a human.

He wakes reluctant. He says nothing for a long time.

Then he tells them.

His bloodline carries a curse laid by a witch, long ago — a dying woman who cursed his ancestors for refusing to save her life. The curse is hereditary and inescapable: if any of his line does anything for another person without charging a price, the transformation begins. He becomes a panther. He must kill something before he can become himself again. The panther bursts out of him. When the curse ends, he bursts out of the panther.

*The reason I leave,* he says, *is that I don't want any of you in the way when it happens.*

The party understands. They had always wondered about the fee-for-everything. Now they know what it costs him to give anything freely.

---

## Where We End

The party makes camp. Shadowdale is ahead.

The next session begins with them arriving.
