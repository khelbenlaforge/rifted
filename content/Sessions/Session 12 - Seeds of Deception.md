## Two Versions of the Same Room

The Sanctum was not finished with them yet.

The party lingered in the chamber, pressing against its mysteries with arcana checks and history rolls. A few things yielded — fragments of cosmological grammar, the shape of what the murals were *saying* without yet giving up what they *meant*. The party had guessed at an exodus, had named it correctly without being able to read it fully. The Sanctum offered no corrections and no confirmations. It simply was.

Then someone looked back at their companions.

[[Balsag]], broad and patient, stood exactly where he had been since they entered. So did [[Erky]]. And [[Little Sister]], the Velvet Vulper who had been inching into the party's affections one session at a time. All three were still. But their eyes were not moving across the murals. Their gaze was fixed on the dragon.

[[Ashardalon]].

The illusion had not dissolved for them.

Where the party saw statuary of four co-creator deities flanking a model of a flat world — where they saw a panoramic mural of divine exodus circling the walls — Balsag and Erky and Little Sister saw the Sunless Citadel as it had been before the cult fell: ancient elven architecture, beautiful in its ruin, and at its center the great illusory dragon that had guarded this sanctum for centuries. They reached out to touch it and felt stone beneath their hands. Ashardalon's presence was *tactile* for them, even in absence.

The murals were invisible. The statues — Mystra, Gond, Bahamut, Takhisis — did not exist in their version of the room.

The Sanctum had revealed itself only to the five from Earth.

## Hang Tight

[[Lea]] reached for the Sending Stone.

She spoke quickly and precisely — a wizard's habit — relaying everything: the door, the trials, the statues, the shape of [[Aenath]], the mural and the featureless figure leading the divine exodus, the gods of the lower planes at the rearguard. Everything they had seen and failed to decode, compressed into the tight syllable-budget of a Sending spell.

[[Luks Borg]]'s reply came back clipped and deliberate, the way his voice always got when he was already thinking three moves ahead:

*"Hang tight. I will get Daria on it."*

Daria. The short form of [[Elmindaria L'Thorian]]. The woman in [[Crystal City]] who had scanned their essence on the day they arrived and had been the first person in [[Aenath]] to take them seriously as anomalies. If Luks was calling her in, he thought what the party had found mattered — mattered at a level above the Sunless Citadel, above [[Sunhold]], above whatever the Hucrele mission had started as.

The Sanctum's secrets were no longer only theirs.

## Northeast

With the Sanctum offering no more immediate answers, the party made a choice. [[Gill Bates]] had wanted southwest. [[Augustus Kennedy]] had wanted northeast since before the door. The vote wasn't close — the Sealed Door had taken the southern route off the table for now, and Augie's impatience was contagious.

Northeast it was.

They returned through the Grove Level's violet bioluminescent corridors, tracking back past the geological fault line that had rent the citadel's lower sections apart, and approached the door they had passed by in Session 11. Through it, they had already clocked the sound: small feet, moving fast. Possibly goblinoid. Possibly kobold.

[[Jonathan Jordan]] put the question to rest.

With the kind of stealth roll that makes the table go quiet, JJ slipped through the door and into the dark — and came back with a map in his head.

The room was a laboratory. Main chamber, long and cluttered. Four rooms branching north. Four more south. Non-combatant goblins, researcher types, moving between workstations with the absorbed focus of people who have forgotten there is an outside world. At the far western end, two armed goblin guards standing before a door — the door, JJ recognized, that connected back to the first room they had descended into when they came down the well from the Upper Level. They had entered the Grove Level at the south. This lab connected to the north.

And between the party's entry point and those two guards: a vine blight in the shape of a great serpent, draped across the workbenches. And beyond it, a swarm of twig blights, twitching in aggregate.

The goblins had no idea the party was watching.

## Freeze

[[Augustus Kennedy]] had a plan.

He held up the horn stolen from [[Durnn]] during the revel — the one [[Ember]] had lifted while the goblin warlord was celebrating with his warriors, too drunk to notice his own pockets being emptied. Durnn's goblins should recognize it. That horn was authority. That horn was *Durnn*, and Durnn was the reason this whole lab existed.

Augie's logic: walk in, hold up the horn, claim to be an envoy. Everyone takes their positions for when it goes wrong.

It went wrong immediately.

The moment Augie stepped into the lab, the blights reacted. The vine serpent uncoiled. The swarm turned as one. Non-combatant goblins scattered into the side rooms and yanked the doors shut. Initiative.

But Augie had committed. He raised the horn, he planted his feet, he shouted with the full conviction of a Paladin of Tyr who was absolutely not improvising:

**"Freeze."**

He rolled deception. He aimed it at the two armed guards at the back.

The armed guards looked at the horn. They looked at each other. They looked at Augie.

They froze.

They did not help during the battle that followed. When Augie asked them afterward — panting, bloodied, the snake blight's paralytic grip still fresh in his memory — why they hadn't intervened, the guards' answer was delivered with complete sincerity and zero awareness of the comedy:

*"You said freeze."*

## The Battle

The vine serpent hit differently than the twig blight swarms they had fought before. The swarm of twig blights was a problem of volume — dozens of snapping, scratching limbs converging at once — but the serpent was a problem of specificity. It *grappled*. It *paralyzed*. When it wrapped around [[Augustus Kennedy]] and held him fast, the paladin who had been directing the entire engagement suddenly became the problem the rest of the party had to solve first.

[[Erky]] was not decorative. The gnome acolyte came through with healing at pressure moments — not tentative half-measures but decisive, powerful interventions, the kind of clerical instinct that saves the turn. [[Little Sister]] was in the fight properly for the first time — not lurking, not waiting to see what happened, but engaged. Something had shifted in her since the party had descended to the Grove Level. She was choosing them now.

[[Balsag]] held his ground with [[Grip]], his surviving giant rat, moving with him as a hunting pair.

The party cleared the room. Vine serpent down. Twig blight swarm dispersed. The two armed guards remained exactly where they had been, frozen, technically compliant.

## The Lab

The researcher goblins came out slowly.

They were, as JJ had surmised, not fighters. They were scientists — in the way that creatures with limited resources and dangerous materials become scientists, which is to say they had developed specific knowledge through direct experimentation and they were absolutely fascinated by their subject matter. They led the party to the workbenches without much prompting. They *wanted* to share.

Here is what the party assembled from the lab's records, the researchers' explanations, and their own checks:

**The supply chain:** [[Belak]] — the druid who had been operating somewhere deeper in the Grove Level, whose name had appeared in letters and been spoken by goblins across multiple sessions — grew the fruit. His plant minions carried it northeast through the door the armed guards had been protecting, and delivered it to [[Durnn]]'s goblins here in the lab. Durnn's people then packaged it and sent merchant teams north, above ground, to sell it to communities throughout the region and further afield. [[Sunhold]] had been one of those communities.

**The terms:** Belak required nothing in return. No gold. No services. No exchange. He wanted the fruit distributed — spread to as many communities as possible, enticing people to travel south toward the Sunless Citadel.

**Durnn's rule:** Despite the arrangement, Durnn had explicitly forbidden his goblins from eating the fruit. The researchers thought this was probably wise. They had noticed things.

**The thing they had noticed:**

The researchers brought the party to a back room.

On a workbench lay a giant rat, opened up. The cut was rough — goblin surgery is not precision work — but the interior was visible. And threading through the organs and muscle tissue, coiling around the ribs, were shoots. Tendrils. Plant matter.

Roots.

The party worked through it together — medicine checks, nature checks, a few failed rolls and one or two that landed — and arrived at a conclusion that sat in the stomach like cold water:

*Eating the fruit while alive is safe.* The seeds enter the body with the flesh, but the living host suppresses them. The fruit heals. The plant waits.

*When the host dies, it stops waiting.*

The body becomes substrate. The seeds germinate. The tendrils that had been biding their time inside muscle and organ find purchase in the dead flesh and begin to grow — and what grows, given enough time, looks remarkably like a twig blight.

The fruit's "healing properties" were real. Belak had not built a lie. He had built something more patient than a lie: a distribution system for sleeping seeds, scattered across dozens of communities, each one waiting inside a living host for the host to eventually, inevitably, die.

The party had been looking for Belak's motivation. They had found it.

## Level Three

Before the long rest, the DM introduced a mechanic the party would be carrying forward.

Borrowing from Brennan Lee Mulligan's approach to campaign pacing — the idea that leveling up is a *narrative* moment as much as a mechanical one — the party was told they could access Level 3 at any point from now. Not all at once, not on a schedule. Whenever a character needed those extra hit points, those new features, that particular feat — the level was there, available to claim.

Even mid-combat, if the moment called for it.

Most of the party leveled up before the long rest began.

## Long Rest

The lab was as safe as anywhere on the Grove Level. The goblin researchers went back to their notes. The armed guards returned to their post by the northwest door. The party settled in.

The northeast door — the one Belak's plant minions came through — waited.

The session ended there, in the hum of violet-lit corridors and the smell of cut fruit and open rat.
