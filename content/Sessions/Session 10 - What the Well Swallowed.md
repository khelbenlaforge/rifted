# Session 10 — What the Well Swallowed

> [!info] Session Details
> **Date Played:**
> **In-Game Date:** Year 222 PA, 16th Marpenoth
> **Location:** [[Sunless Citadel]], Durbuluk Goblin Territory → Grove Level
> **Party:** [[Jonathan Jordan]], [[Gill Bates]], [[Ember]], [[Augustus Kennedy]], [[Lea]]
> **Companions:** [[Erky]] (rescued this session), [[Little Sister]] (rescued this session), [[Balsag]] (joined this session), Grip (giant rat, Balsag's companion)

---

## The Cages

Morning in the [[Durbuluk Goblins]]' territory arrived without light and without particular warmth. The party had rested in the goblin pantry, waiting for [[Durnn]].

What they found first were the cages.

Two of them, side by side. One held a gnome — small, road-worn, clearly alive. The other held something stranger: a fox-like creature with midnight blue, velvety fur and multiple tails, the kind of creature that belongs in a fae glade rather than a goblin dungeon. When addressed in Sylvan, the creature answered to *Little Sister* — a name that carried the weight of something given rather than chosen.

The gnome was [[Erky]] — [[Tymora]]'s acolyte from [[Sunhold]], sent south by [[Sister Garaele]] to find the Hucrele children and captured here instead, kept as some combination of curiosity and contingency. He had survived. He was not particularly dignified about it, but survival rarely is.

[[Grenl]] was persuaded without great difficulty. She was the woman who had watched her fellow elders die one by one and made her peace with decisions she could not undo; adding two prisoners to a party she had already admitted was not the kind of thing she would spend political capital resisting. The cages were opened. [[Erky]] and [[Little Sister]] joined the company.

The [[Velvet Vulper]]s, it turned out, were fox-like fae — many-tailed, dark as a midnight forest, strange in the way fae things are strange: entirely present and somehow not quite here.

## A Message Through the Stone

That evening, still in goblin territory, [[Lea]] used the Sending Stone to reach [[Luks Borg]].

She relayed the shape of things: the citadel, the grove, the companions they had acquired. Luks listened.

His response arrived in measured pieces, the way Luks always communicated when he had decided something without telling you he had decided something. He was glad about [[Erky]]. He was specifically glad in a way that warranted a separate instruction: *keep him close. Don't let him know you're watching him.*

[[Lea]] did not ask why. Luks's directives were rarely improved by interrogation. She filed it beside the other things she was carrying that had no frame yet.

---

## The Fruit and the Man

In the space between waiting and acting, the party traded information with the goblins — or rather, listened to what the goblins were willing to say.

The [[Goblin Fruit]] had a clearer origin now: it came from [[Belak]], the druid somewhere below. He sent it up to [[Durnn]], who distributed it and traded it to communities aboveground — to [[Sunhold]] and beyond. Belak grew it. Durnn moved it. The goblins received something in exchange; what exactly, they did not specify. The arrangement had the flavor of a supply chain more than a gift, which said something about everyone involved.

---

## The Procession

Cheers broke the waiting.

[[Ragh]] entered the goblin territory carrying the unconscious body of [[Yusdrayl]] — the [[Ordahir Kobolds]]' queen, the pragmatic sorcerer who had managed her colony through political performance and sheer determination, who had never fully believed in the mythology she wielded, and who had not expected it to end here.

He carried her the way a soldier carries a standard: forward, deliberate, in full view. The goblins erupted.

[[Durnn]] emerged from elsewhere to join the procession — the goblin leader who had killed his own elders to take power, who had been secretly working with Ragh for longer than anyone but the two of them knew. He led what followed: a victory parade through goblin territory toward the Throne Room, goblins and kobolds temporarily sharing the same cheer because the thing they had both been building toward had finally arrived.

---

## The Well

The Throne Room's centerpiece was not a throne.

A large shaft dropped through the floor — not a pit but a constructed well, its walls lined with glowing purple moss that pulsed faintly in the dark. The bottom was roughly a hundred feet down. Sound carried strangely in its depth.

[[Durnn]] did not make a speech. He did not invoke any god or name any cause. He walked to the edge and dropped [[Yusdrayl]] into the shaft without ceremony, and then turned back to the room.

A hundred feet below, the woman who had governed the [[Ordahir Kobolds]] by the strength of her performance ended her performance.

---

## The Revel

Durnn produced alcohol from somewhere, and the celebration began.

[[Augustus Kennedy]] joined the drinking. He remained upright throughout — his resistance to poison extending, apparently, to the goblins' conception of a victory toast. Durnn and most of the warrior goblins became comprehensively intoxicated. [[Augustus]] was sober inside it, watching.

Elsewhere, [[Lea]], [[Gill Bates]], and [[Ember]] used the cover of the revel to speak with [[Grenl]]. She provided what she could — not warmth, never warmth, but information offered in the dry tone of someone who has been keeping things to herself for long enough that giving them away feels like release.

---

## What Ember Took

[[Ember]] moved through the celebration with a druid's economy of motion: unhurried, deliberate, already at the next place before anyone registered she had left the current one.

From [[Ragh]]'s person, heavily intoxicated and unguarded, she lifted *Toothpick* — his named blade, the weapon cleaned too carefully for the skirmish frequency he described, the one he kept sharp for reasons that were personal rather than tactical.

From [[Durnn]], she took a horn. It radiated magic.

She did not announce either acquisition.

---

## Into the Dark

With the revel continuing around them and [[Grenl]] indifferent to their departure — or rather, watching with the expression of someone who has decided not to be a problem — the party returned to the shaft.

They dropped a rope.

[[Erky]] and [[Little Sister]] descended with them. The gnome acolyte with more courage than sense and the fae creature with her midnight fur and too many tails, both committed to the company now in the way that surviving together commits you to people.

The goblins above ceased to matter.

---

## The Body at the Bottom

At the base of the shaft: [[Yusdrayl]].

She was not dead in the way of someone at peace. She was dead in the way of a hundred-foot fall, on stone, without ceremony. Her robes had not made the journey gracefully. The queen who had kept the [[Ordahir Kobolds]] together through sheer political will lay at the bottom of the well her enemies had built for her.

The party noted her. Noted the fact of her. And moved into the Grove.

---

## Violet Light

The [[Sunless Citadel]]'s lower level did not look like what it sounded.

Bioluminescent moss covered the walls — violet, bright enough to read by, pulsing gently in cycles that had nothing to do with day or night. The citadel opened here into something that had once been cultivated and was now wild in the way abandoned cultivation goes wild: organized shapes still faintly visible beneath the growth, the bones of an intentional space taken back by something older.

It was beautiful in the way that things are beautiful when they have stopped pretending to be anything.

---

## Balsag

The plant creatures came out of the moss.

Not attacking the party — attacking a bugbear. Large, gruff, slightly baffled by the turn events had taken, beset on multiple sides by things that moved wrong. One of his giant rats was down. The other pressed close, alive, named [[Grip]], holding.

The party moved in.

After the fight, the bugbear introduced himself: [[Balsag]]. He had been the keeper of the dragon [[Calcryx]] — the wyrmling the entire Upper Level had been fighting about, the one whose kidnapping had set Ragh's plan in motion. He had gone out through the Underdark tunnels beneath the citadel searching for food — for Calcryx, who needed feeding — and had become lost. The tunnels had deposited him, eventually, here. He had been in the Grove since, disoriented and increasingly beset by whatever the plant creatures were.

He was good-natured about it, in the gruff way of someone who has not yet decided to be upset.

He and [[Grip]] joined the party without further negotiation. Two more creatures with no better option and no particular hesitation about the company they were keeping.
