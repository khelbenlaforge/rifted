## The Life That Kept Going

The Sanctum mural is still glowing.

[[Ember]] and [[Augustus Kennedy]] join the vision last, and they enter it the way everyone does: by watching themselves, at a distance, living a life they are no longer inside.

[[Augustus Kennedy]] is a Brooklyn cop on an ordinary morning. Donuts. Bodega coffee. A dispatch call about a friend making a scene — drunk on the sidewalk, open container, getting loud outside a place Augie loves. The friend sees Augie's badge, his family, his *perfect life*, and starts unpacking something much older than today. On cue: Augie's wife calling about a kid who lashed out at school. A perfectly ordinary day, from the outside. A man held upright by small frictions and minor emergencies.

[[Ember]] is at work. An animal shelter in the middle of its usual beautiful chaos. A regular volunteer walks in with a crate and something is moving inside it that has no business being in the city. Nat 20. It is a pangolin. Simultaneously, Animal Control calls to say an inspector will be there in fifteen minutes. She tries to talk them down. Her assistant bursts out of the back room and at full volume announces the pangolin's presence to the room — and to the person on the phone, who immediately starts asking questions.

Both of them realize, separately, that they are not *in* these moments. They are watching. The same remove JJ and Gill and Lea felt last session: the gap they left, filled in by echo.

Then a woosh, and a door, and somewhere else.

## The Space Between Sessions

While [[Augie]] and [[Ember]] floated in the void, [[Jonathan Jordan]], [[Gill Bates]], and [[Lea]] were in Arabel, riding toward Castle Kilgrave, standing before Helm, watching [[Mystra]] burn.

That felt like days. For Augie and Ember, it was nothing. When the prequel party finally sleeps at the end of Session 13, all five ethereal forms drift together in the same liminal space — and the moment of reunion arrives like two conversations colliding. JJ and Gill and Lea are calm. They have the thousand-yard stare of people who have witnessed something and have not fully named it yet. Augie is immediately suspicious of himself: *Is this my fault? I touched the wall.* Yes. Gently — yes.

The debrief happens in the dark between worlds. Magic does not work here. The story is laid out. Mystra is dead. Helm is standing between the gods and heaven. The Tablets of Fate are somewhere in the Realms, and they are needed — not by the gods, not by the church, but by something larger. Someone hands the mission to the mortals in the room.

*Find Elminster. Find the Tablets. Go.*

The DM pulls the avatars back down. Augie and Ember follow.

## Before the Fire

*The campfire scene comes first.*

This happens before the party reaches [[Shadowdale]] — a closing stitch on Session 13's loose thread. The previous night, [[Kelemvor]] slipped away from camp and came back the way he always does: unconscious, bloody, someone else's dinner beside him. The party used *Command* to make him talk. He talked.

He is a man from [[Arabel]] who has charged for everything his whole life because the alternative costs him everything else. The curse is hereditary. A Lyonsbane ancestor let a hag die without lifting a finger to help her, and in her dying breath she laid the price on every one of his line: *you cannot do anything for another person — truly, freely, without a transaction attached — without losing yourself.* Kelemvor has spent years building the perfect cage for it. His detachment is not cruelty. His fees are not greed. His entire personality is a prosthetic.

*This is my problem,* he says. *This is my curse.*

[[Torm of the Dales]] tells him they understand. They're not leaving him behind. The party that found each other in a mercenary city in Cormyr — Kelemvor and [[Cyric]] oldest, then Torm, then [[Adon]], then [[Midnight]] last — has survived stranger revelations than this.

They ride north.

## A Harper at the Post

[[Selmavra Elsree]] has been operating in the Shadowdale region for close to ten years.

She knows every guard rotation, every checkpoint, every captain who will be bribed and every one who won't. She and [[Vesper]] — male, rescued from animal slavers while still bleeding from a wound, now large and calm and watchful — have become part of the furniture at the Shadowdale guard post. The guards have stopped flinching at the wolf. Some of them feed him treats.

[[Riothar Irontongue]], the Guardscaptain, gives her the morning report: Scout Team A has spotted dark-armored soldiers pillaging towns to the north and west. He names them. **[[Zhentarim]].**

She keeps her face neutral. She has been waiting for this.

Then she spots them — five figures approaching from a mile out, reading wrong, moving like people carrying something recent and heavy. She knows what she is looking at even at that distance. She's been doing this long enough to read bodies like maps. The one in the lead — the gait, the posture, the way his eyes move — she has seen that walk before, from a distance, from a rooftop, from behind a market stall. She knows a Harper when she sees one.

She does not react. [[Vesper]] growls softly. She puts her hand on his back. *Easy.*

## Through the Gates

The party arrives. Guardscaptain Riothar does what Guardscaptains do: *Names and business.*

[[Midnight]] says they are from Arabel. Just passing through. Buying supplies. Riothar accepts it, the way people accept things that are probably not true but aren't yet lies. She's good at this.

Those who listen carefully hear something else — a guard, quiet, telling Riothar that Aldith says the north road has been quiet since dawn. *That's not quiet. That's an army not moving yet.* A woman with a market basket exchanges a wordless look with a guard and says, almost to herself: *more of them. Good.* Shadowdale is a town that has learned to read incoming weather.

Riothar assigns [[Hawksguard]] to escort the party. Reports of armed soldiers in the area, he says, gesturing vaguely at the horizon. Nothing to worry about.

Hawksguard doesn't look like he believes that. But he leads them in anyway.

---

## The Tower on the Edge of Town

Shadowdale prepares rather than panics. Provisions are moving. The blacksmith is on double shift. The Temple of [[Tymora]] is being cleared out — some part of the town already knows it will become a field hospital before long. The people who clock the party at the gate assess them the same way: not a threat, not a burden, possibly useful. The town has been here before.

Hawksguard names the landmarks as they walk. He deflects questions about troop movements. *Harvest season. Nothing to worry about.* His voice says something different.

An Arcana check clocks it before [[Torm]] says anything: there is a tower on the edge of town that radiates power like a hearth radiates heat. Subtle to the untrained eye. Not subtle at all to anyone who knows what magic at rest smells like.

*Oh,* says [[Torm]], in the voice of someone who has been meaning to mention this. *That's my friend Elminster's place.*

The party stares at him. Hawksguard stares at him. There is a beat of very specific silence.

*You've known Elminster for a couple years, you've been to Shadowdale before, you've been sent on jobs by him — and you didn't think to mention this?*

[[Midnight]], subsequently, casts *Command* (Chaos roll: 57, nothing unusual). He confirms everything. He is telling the truth. He is omitting context. There is a difference between the two, and Torm is very comfortable with that difference.

Hawksguard doubles his pace. He hustles them toward the inn.

## The Old Skull Inn

[[Jhaele Silvermane]] greets [[Selmavra]]'s skirmish unit first — the shift ending, everything in order. Then she clocks the five of them, and something flickers across her face as she looks at [[Torm]]. She almost says something. Catches herself. She pours wine instead.

She knows everyone, Jhaele. She knows [[Rathan Thentraver]] by first name. She knows [[Elminster]] as "that old fuddy-duddy, once he gets his head stuck in something you can't pull him out for meals." She knows this town the way you know a place you have been responsible for, quietly, for a very long time. Her bearing is not innkeeper affect. It is the bearing of someone who has seen far grander rooms and preferred this one.

[[Adon]] makes no effort to conceal that the Old Skull Inn does not meet his standards. Jhaele clocks his holy symbol — Sune, goddess of beauty — and serves him a crystal carafe of expensive wine without being asked. *Your Majesty. Wine it is.* He accepts this as his due.

[[Kelemvor]] wants information. He frames it correctly: *I'll buy you a drink, you tell me about the wizard.* Persuasion 18, advantage. Hawksguard takes the drink. [[Elminster]] is the reason Shadowdale is safe, he says. One of the most powerful mages in the Forgotten Realms. The lord of the town is a man named [[Mourngrym Amcathra]]. Hawksguard doesn't volunteer more than that.

At a Perception roll of 15 in the inn: dark armor. Dark-armored soldiers, already inside, watching the room. The net is tightening and has been for longer than the party realized.

## Michael C. Jordan

[[Selmavra]] is piecing it together.

She caught the behavioral tells outside the gate — the specific way these five move *with* each other rather than alongside each other, the unspoken triangulations, the shared reference frame they carry like a second skin. Her nat 20 at the guard post crystallized it: the raven-haired mage is probably [[Lea]]. The man with the Harper's walk is probably [[JJ]]. She doesn't know the others yet. And she can't signal directly — [[Cyric]] is at the table, and Cyric is always watching.

She finds a way in.

She mentions, casually, that she almost named [[Vesper]] "Michael C. Jordan." She'd heard the name from someone — someone who'd known a "Michael B. Jordan" whose rat was named Michael C. Jordan. Strange story. She'd always liked the name.

The table goes quiet in the particular way of people who have heard a password in a language they didn't know anyone else spoke.

[[Torm]] finds his opening: *Augie would be a great name for a familiar. Gill also, actually.*

[[Cyric]] watches this with narrow eyes and decides they are being strange, which is not unusual for people who have recently survived a god fight. Cover holds. He goes back to measuring exits.

## The Courtyard

They leave the inn to walk to Elminster's tower.

The courtyard is large: cobblestones, stables, a stable boy too young to be working that hard, horses being taken in for the evening. It should be a crossing, not a confrontation.

A supply cart barrels through the gate at speed. The stable boy has his back turned.

[[Midnight]] sees it before [[Kelemvor]] can react. She shouts across the courtyard: *I'll give you a silver to save him.*

He saves him. Transaction intact. Curse does not trigger.

The Zhentarim were already inside the courtyard.

They had been there before the party arrived — pre-positioned, waiting for an opening. The cart chaos is the opening. While perception is split, the Agent moves: he's behind Midnight, arm around her throat, dragging her toward the gate. *We have her. Cover me.* The Commander plants himself at the exit — a wall, not yet a weapon. Five Toughs swarm [[Kelemvor]], blades out, trying to keep him occupied.

[[Kelemvor]] sees [[Midnight]] being dragged away.

He does not say *how much.* He does not say *what's it worth to you.* He turns and attacks the Agent. To save her. Just that.

The curse doesn't care about intent.

---

## The Panther in the Courtyard

It takes two rounds. Then the clothes shred and the cobblestones are red and what is standing in [[Kelemvor]]'s place is not Kelemvor.

[[Cyric]] reacts in one heartbeat: sees it, calculates it as a threat, stabs it twice. Cold. Silent. No hesitation and no cruelty — pure assessment. He didn't know what it was. He saw something dangerous near someone he travels with. He acted accordingly.

[[Torm]] puts a crossbow bolt through the Agent's throat. Crit. [[Midnight]] is free.

[[Selmavra]] casts *Hail of Thorns*. The chaos rebound doubles back — she takes eight damage herself. She shakes it off and keeps moving.

[[Jhaele Silvermane]] appears in the inn doorway on round two with a crossbow leveled at the Commander. *Do not touch children in my presence.* The tone is not a warning. It is a correction.

The Commander grabs the stable boy anyway. Living shield, sword at his throat. [[Midnight]] casts *Command* ("Release"). He releases him. The Chaos table fires again — Reverse Gravity. Midnight is launched into the air. Her pendant activates: the shard of [[Mystra]]'s power responds to genuine danger on its own, and she floats back down like someone has reached out a hand from above to catch her.

[[Torm]] goes down. Gets revived. Comes back up.

[[Vesper]] fights throughout. Takes significant damage. Stays between the stable boy and whatever is nearest.

Mid-combat, the DM calls a ten-minute break. Everyone levels to 4. The Deferred Level-Up rule applies; anyone who hasn't claimed their advance does so now. Midnight adds one point to Intelligence. Her INT is now 18, spell DC now 14. The fight resumes.

The Lyonsbane Panther does not kill. It circles, threatens, disrupts, attacks — but for most of the fight it holds off finishing anyone. The party watches it route the last Tough toward the corner of the courtyard, and everyone understands at once that they are watching something make a choice.

The Tough goes down. The curse breaks.

Something leaves the panther. Separates, like a soul from a fever. Kelemvor is on the cobblestones — naked, unconscious, blood on the stone around him that is not all his.

The courtyard is quiet.

## Where We End

The party won. No one is dead. The stable boy is alive and staring at all of them from behind a post. [[Jhaele Silvermane]] has not lowered her crossbow yet.

The cipher note on the Commander, [[Vael]]'s appearance above the stable roof, the loot from the Zhentarim dead — all of it is still out there. Elminster's tower is still lit. [[Mourngrym Amcathra]] is still lord of this town and does not know these people exist.

The next session picks up in the aftermath.
