## Grain Sacks and Cobblestones

[[Kelemvor Lyonsbane|Kelemvor]] wakes up naked on the courtyard stones with no memory of the last ten minutes.

This is the arrangement: [[Augustus Kennedy]] is conscious, passenger inside a body that was recently a panther, and Kelemvor is present the way a man is present after a high fever breaks — awake, confused, in possession of his name but not much else. Augi is watching from the inside. The stones are cold. There is blood nearby that is not all from the Zhentarim.

He finds two grain sacks and makes the best of it.

Across the courtyard, something drops from the rooftop ledge and lands without sound. A figure — tall, dark-haired, composed, looking at the aftermath the way an architect looks at a building she is already redesigning in her head. Her eyes move to [[Selmavra Elsree]]. She has the look of someone who has been watching for a while and found the watch instructive.

*"Jumping into danger for strangers? Fascinating."*

Then she goes invisible. She does not leave.

[[Vael]] has been here the whole time. She will continue to be here.

## Operation Shadowfall

Six bodies. One cipher note.

The Commander's coat has a hidden lining — someone who knew what they were doing sewed it there. The note comes out in fragments: *Operation Shadowfall. Pendant bearer: acquire or neutralize. Carries fragment of divine intelligence. Value: CRITICAL.* And below that, in colder language: *Lyonsbane Rider. Do not engage. Draw out and observe.* Reports to Voonlar. Agent designation: Cray.

The loot is real — a Potion of Heroism and a Scroll of Misty Step off the Commander, distributed without ceremony. The note is more interesting than the potions.

[[Midnight]] and [[Selmavra Elsree|Selmavra]] work the cipher together. Midnight is quick — too quick, maybe. She hands back the decoded note and there is something in the way she holds the paper that suggests the version the group sees is not the entire version she read.

She is not lying. She is editing. There is a difference, and Midnight is very comfortable with that difference.

[[Jhaele Silvermane]] appears in the doorway, crossbow finally at her side. Her face is the face of someone calculating not whether she is angry but at what scale.

*"The Zhentarim used my courtyard as a staging post."* A pause. *"Those exact words."* Accounts will be settled with [[Mourngrym Amcathra]] for the damage. She is not asking.

## What They Know Between Worlds

Somewhere the PCs are not — the gray space between conscious and story, the room with no walls where their original minds keep finding each other — there is a powwow.

[[Lea]], [[Ember]], [[Augustus Kennedy]], [[Gill Bates]], [[Jonathan Jordan]]: five people from Earth who are also, currently, five people in Shadowdale, sitting in a courtyard full of bodies. In the liminal space it is quieter. Lea lays out more of the cipher than Midnight shared. The group turns the new information over.

They make History checks on the names. The picture assembles: two mages at the top of the world, standing on opposite poles. [[Elminster]], wanderer and sage, counsel to kings and servant of Mystra. [[Khelben Blackstaff]], Archmage of Waterdeep, institutional and deliberate, reaching through proxies and careful structures. And on the other side of the coin: [[Manshoon]], from [[Zhentil Keep]], the dark counterweight — power exercised without the pretense of service to anything greater than itself.

One of them rolls a natural 20.

The name that comes with it is a name none of them should know, not here, not in this body, not in this year. Khelben Blackstaff has a son. His name is **Corran Blackstaff**.

The name lands differently than it should. Because in Aenath — in the world they were pulled from, in the world that remade itself 222 years ago around a sacrifice no one fully understands — that name belongs to the archmage who entombed himself in the rock to stop the world from tearing. The founding mage of a world that shouldn't exist, built with blood drawn from the bloodline of one of Faerûn's greatest wizards.

They carry this forward like a stone in a pocket. The vision doesn't pause for it. It never does.

## The Walk and the Shadow at Its Shoulder

The road to [[Elminster]]'s tower is short and uneventful.

[[Vael]] walks it at [[Selmavra Elsree|Selmavra]]'s shoulder — invisible, present, close enough that Selmavra can feel the faint displacement in the air beside her. She does not address this. [[Vael]] does not address it. The arrangement is whatever it is.

The tower announces itself before they arrive: warm light in an upper window, a pond visible through the trees, the smell of pipeweed on the air. It is not an impressive structure for the power it contains. It is the sort of building that suggests the person inside has better things to think about than architecture.

## The Sage at the Pond

[[Elminster]] is sitting at the edge of the pond with his feet in the water. Pipe lit. Hat at a familiar angle. He is looking at the surface of the water as though it said something worth considering.

[[Storm Silverhand]] stands at the treeline, watching. Not the trees. The shadows between them.

[[Rathan Thentraver]] is eating an apple on a garden wall.

Storm's eyes go straight to [[Midnight]] the moment the party rounds the hedge. No greeting. No announcement. Just a look, and in return a look, and something passes between them that has no name attached to it. [[Midnight]]'s Insight is high enough to catch what Storm is hiding: behind the neutrality, behind the deliberate stillness, there is grief. Old grief. The kind that has learned to stay quiet.

Rathan drops off the wall and opens his arms.

*"Two silver, Torm."* It is not a greeting. It is an inside joke from a long time ago and it lands exactly the way inside jokes land between people who have been in danger together — like a door opening, like a light coming on. [[Torm of the Dales]] walks into the hug without hesitation, and Rathan holds him at arm's length after and looks at him with a grin that finds nothing wrong behind the eyes. Nothing off. Nothing suspicious.

The PCs are acting as their characters. They have always been acting as their characters. The people who know those characters see exactly who they expect to see.

Elminster turns his head without standing up.

*"Took ye long enough. Sit down, you're blocking the light."*

A bench materializes. Not conjured from nothing — it assembles itself from the environment, shaped from what was already there, and it is exactly right: suited to the garden, aesthetically pleasing, the kind of object that suggests the person who made it did so with genuine attention. [[Adon]] inspects it. He approves.

Elminster points his pipe at [[Torm of the Dales|Torm]]: *"Did ye, at any point this evening, attempt to pick someone's pocket during an armed altercation?"*

## The Shape of the World

He tells them what he knows. He does it efficiently, in the manner of a man who has explained things to people for a very long time and has learned which details they actually need.

**Ao the Overgod** stripped the gods of their power. Not diminished them — removed it. They walk in mortal flesh now, carrying their domains like a weight they can barely lift. Magic is unstable because the source of magic is unstable. **The Tablets of Fate** were stolen, and without them the divine compact that ordered everything cannot be restored. One tablet is in Tantras, in Dragon Reach, to the east. That is where this road eventually leads.

Beneath the **Temple of Lathander**, there is a **Celestial Stairway**. Invisible to most. A road out of Faerûn for gods who can find it. **[[Bane]]** is coming for it — not because he wants to leave, but because it gives him leverage, and because the power in [[Midnight]]'s pendant is specifically what he wants to carry through it.

**[[Helm]]** is uniquely unaffected. He alone retained his divine power, and no one knows why.

Storm raises her hand. Silver fire runs across her palm like it has always been there, waiting to be introduced.

Elminster does the same.

*"All of us,"* he says. *"All of us have a piece of Mystra."* Not just Midnight. Multiple carriers. The goddess scattered herself when she died, and the shards landed in people who could hold them.

The plan, as plans go, is elegant in principle and terrible in practice: do not kill Bane. Another dead god worsens the instability. Mystra's death has already doubled the chaos in the weave. What they need is to neutralize him — to stop him reaching the Stairway, stop him using the pendant's power, stop the war he is currently executing on three fronts while this conversation takes place.

Bane's followers can sacrifice their own souls to fuel him. A faithful army marching toward you is not just a military problem. It is a theological one.

## What Storm Knows

[[Storm Silverhand]] takes out her instrument without ceremony and starts playing.

The song is not for listening to. It is a ward — woven music, precision-cast, designed for exactly one purpose: to block every scrying spell, every divination, every searching eye that is currently reaching through the weave looking for a specific pendant. The song closes around [[Midnight]] like a door.

[[Rathan Thentraver|Rathan]] explains [[Torm of the Dales|Torm]] while Torm pretends not to be listening.

The Knights of Myth Drannor are the guardians of an ancient elven city that destroyed itself — the elves overreached, the way elves occasionally do, and the city became a den of something older and worse. The Knights hold the perimeter. The Ninth regiment is on its way back from the east. And Torm is one of them — has been for years, a fact he mentioned to nobody in Shadowdale's tavern or Shadowdale's courtyard or at any point during the evening's festivities.

*"He's the only one who keeps this place lively,"* Rathan says. It is a compliment. It is also absolutely true.

## The Lord's Table

[[Mourngrym Amcathra]] runs his war council over a feast, which says something about him: a man who understands that people think more clearly when they have eaten, or who has seen enough sieges to know that hot food is a form of morale.

The map has three colored stones. North: **the Bloody Doom**, pressing on Tower Farm — the town militia is holding, barely. East: **the Claw of Voonlar**, at the crossroads, where the [[The Harpers|Harpers]] are contesting the advance. West: **the West Fang**, moving toward Ashaba Bridge — but the west flank is the one [[Selmavra Elsree|Selmavra]]'s Harpers have been watching, and the intelligence is fragmentary. They have movement sightings. They do not have numbers.

The man behind all of it is **[[Manshoon]]** — legendary wizard-lord of the [[Zhentarim]], author of this battle plan, currently at [[Zhentil Keep]] while his battle lords execute the work. He did not come himself. Whether that is confidence or caution, Mourngrym does not say.

His wizards attempt a scry. They succeed: the western Battlelord identified, position fixed. Mourngrym offers each of them the rank of Sword — above his guardscaptains — and twenty gold a day.

They accept.

It matters, the commission. Not just tactically. [[Kelemvor Lyonsbane|Kelemvor]] fighting under official orders, in service to a lord, for Shadowdale rather than for personal gain — that is not the same as a mercenary taking coin for blood. The distinction is one the curse understands. He files it away.

The party deploys to Ashaba Bridge with [[Storm Silverhand]], [[Rathan Thentraver|Rathan]], and [[Torm of the Dales|Torm]].

## The West Fang

The drums arrive before the soldiers do — felt through the soles of the boots before they are heard. The West Fang crossing turns out to be approximately fifty infantry plus one Battlelord, and the battle that follows is not clean but it is decisive.

This is mass combat: each turn takes down more than one. [[Selmavra Elsree|Selmavra]] drops Hunter's Mark on the Battlelord and tracks him through the press. Storm takes out nine in a single pass. Someone throws a Fog Cloud on the enemy rear — a blinding wall that collapses their formation just long enough for the flanks to close. [[Vesper]] fights alongside [[Cyric]], two animals that don't trust each other and both know it, which makes them efficient.

[[Midnight]] lands a Tasha's Mind Whip on the Battlelord — twelve psychic damage — and the Chaos table fires. The damage divides. It finds targets the spell wasn't aimed at. She watches it scatter and files that information for later.

The cavalry arrives on the ninth phase. What remains of the West Fang breaks, routes, and is gone. The bridge holds.

## The Ninth

A second large force arrives on the far side of the bridge while the first is still being cleared.

The party turns to face it. They are down resources and the Battlelord is still standing somewhere in the scrum. It is the kind of moment where the math stops working in your favor.

Then the banners come. Silver moon. Harp. The Knights of Myth Drannor, arriving at speed — not because they were dispatched, but because the Ninth regiment was already moving, already converging, and the sound of the battle reached them first.

[[Florin Falconhand]] does not have time for ceremony. He grabs [[Rathan Thentraver|Rathan]] by the collar and tosses him into the fray the way you pass a weapon to someone who needs it. [[Dove Falconhand]] flanks without being asked. [[Jhessail Silvertree]] drops a Fireball that touches no allies. [[Lanseril Snowmantle]] calls lightning down the column. [[Merith Strongbow]] finds the officers.

[[Illistyl Elventree]] finds [[Torm of the Dales|Torm]].

*"You're alive."*

She says it the way people say things they have been holding in one hand for a very long time. Then she raises the other hand and launches him into the fray with a force spell — and in the exact moment he leaves the ground, she kisses him on the cheek.

He flies. He is smiling.

## What the Temple Holds

Word arrives mid-battle from a runner who comes in too fast to be good news: an elite Zhentarim force is bearing down on the **Temple of Lathander**. Not the bridge. Not the crossroads. The Temple.

The Temple sits between the party and that force. And within that force — someone who can feel these things says it plainly — there is something that is not soldiers. Something that was once a god.

The debate is brief. They cannot take [[Bane]]'s avatar in the open, not with depleted resources, not with an elite force behind him. Retreat into the Temple. Use the walls. Use whatever time they can buy.

[[Elminster]] is already inside when they arrive. He bars the doors. The Celestial Stairway — invisible before — glows through the stone floor like something subterranean and enormous, finally announced. [[Midnight]]'s pendant responds: warmth, from something that recognizes something.

*"Now we wait."*

Six turns. They use all of them.

---

## The Doors Break

The Zhentarim don't knock.

Six infantry and a Battlelord pour through the shattered doors while Elminster stands near the Stairway with absolute concentration — not a word, not a glance at the party, all of it focused on something only he can see. Whatever he is trying to do with the Stairway, he is not going to stop doing it. Not for this.

The Battlelord casts **Spirit Guardians** and the temple fills with hostile radiance that follows him everywhere. The six infantry go directly for the party. This is a diversion. Everyone understands that immediately. The Battlelord is keeping them occupied until the door behind him opens wider.

---

## The Black Lord

Round five.

Nothing enters through the broken doors. Nothing visible.

Then a specific cold fills the room — the kind that is not temperature but is understood by the body as temperature, a cold that means *something has entered this space that is much larger than the space*. The Battlelord sheathes his sword. The kneeling soldiers go flat. Not ordered to. Not signaled. They simply stop.

[[Bane]] teleports into the centre of the temple. Eight feet tall. Black plate. The gauntlet symbol. Red eyes that have no business being in a mortal face. He appears exactly halfway between the party and the Stairway — halfway between [[Elminster]] and the people trying to keep [[Midnight]] from being taken.

He looks at the Stairway. Then at Midnight. Then at Elminster.

Nothing is said. It is the silence of a man who has already decided.

---

## Two Time Stops and a Wish

Elminster does not wait.

Bane opens with **Meteor Swarm** — four columns of fire and stone, targeted on a single old man — and Elminster is already moving before they land. **Time Stop.** Four extra moves. The duel is not turn-based anymore. The duel is *Elminster filling every second he steals with everything he has*, spending resources that took decades to accumulate, spending them like they are infinite because right now they have to be.

Bane takes his own Time Stop. Retaliates. **Power Word Kill** is in the air. Elminster keeps working.

**Wish** buys a third Time Stop. More spells. Disintegrate. Chains of light. A combat that looks, from the outside, like two men standing very still and the world exploding around them in intervals — teleporting, countering, the Stairway growing brighter with every exchange as whatever Elminster is trying to do to block it falls incrementally further behind what Bane is trying to do to open it.

[[Midnight]] is being pulled toward the Stairway. Not by anything she can see. The pendant knows where it came from, and the Stairway is calling it home.

The party holds her. Four of them, hands on her arms, her shoulders, her coat — anchoring her the way you anchor a door in a storm. The Stairway pulls. They pull back.

## Be Brave

Round eight. Round nine.

[[Elminster]] drops to one knee. Smoke rises from his coat — not from the spells. From him. His eyes find [[Midnight]]'s pendant, which is now glowing brilliantly, filling the temple with a light that has no color the eye can agree on.

He looks at her.

*"Be brave, lass. Take him down."*

---

## She Remembers

The pendant ignites.

[[Midnight]] is not Midnight anymore — or she is entirely Midnight, more Midnight than she has ever been, more than the woman who was born in Arabel and studied magic in secret and carried a shard of a dead goddess for reasons she didn't fully understand. She is wild-haired and glowing and made of blue-white starmotes, and the voice that comes out of her is not just hers.

*"You killed me once. I thought you should know — I remember."*

She binds herself to [[Bane]]. Not a spell. Something older than spells. She does not fight him. She wraps around him, tethered, and begins to pull — toward the Stairway, toward the door out of the world — and she is **smiling**.

Somewhere in the chaos, a masonry shard from the crumbling east wall catches [[Adon]] across the face. It flies under the radar. He notices the blood on his fingers only after. It will leave a scar. Permanent. A cleric of [[Sune]] — the goddess of beauty — marked permanently across the face in the wreckage of a temple, in the moment a dead goddess chose to die again on purpose.

Nobody sees it happen. There is too much light.

---

## The Explosion

The east wall blasts outward.

Then there is sky where the wall was, and the Stairway is gone — the glow is gone, the warmth is gone, the sense of something enormous and directional beneath the floor is simply absent, the way sound is absent after a bell stops ringing. [[Bane]] is gone. [[Elminster]] is gone. [[Mystra]] is gone.

All five PCs are on the floor.

The pendant in [[Midnight]]'s hand is cold. For the first time since she found it, it is just a pendant — small and dark and empty, the fragment spent, the piece of a goddess that lived in it given away.

Outside, through the hole in the wall, Shadowdale is still standing. Smoke on the horizon, but the drums have stopped.

## You Will Stand Trial

[[Sharantyr]] enters through the broken doors with Harpers behind her, swords drawn. She came through the rubble. She found strangers standing in the wreckage where Elminster had been. She found his hat on the floor, and his pipe, and his staff — laid out like punctuation.

The math looks simple.

*"You will stand trial for the murder of Elminster, Sage of Shadowdale."*

She gets to the word *Shadowdale* —

## The Citadel

— and the vision fractures.

Five pairs of eyes, blinking in the dark. Stone walls. A mural that is no longer glowing. The familiar smell of old stone and underground cold, and the weight of Level 3 settling back into bones that were briefly Level 4.

Durnn's Research Lab. The Sunless Citadel around them, unchanged, patient.

They have seen Mystra die. They have watched an old man hold off a god so a young woman could finish the job. They carry the knowledge that the man who built their world was the son of a Faerûnian archmage who came into the chaos knowing exactly what he was there to do.

They are back.
