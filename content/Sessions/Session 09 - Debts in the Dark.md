# Session 09 — Debts in the Dark

> [!info] Session Details
> **Date Played:**
> **In-Game Date:** Year 222 PA, 15th Marpenoth
> **Location:** [[Sunless Citadel]], Upper Level → Durbuluk Goblin Territory
> **Party:** [[Jonathan Jordan]], [[Gill Bates]], [[Ember]], [[Augustus Kennedy]], [[Lea]]
> **Companions:** [[Meepo]] (guide, Upper Level only)

## Letters Before Dawn

The party slept in the main rest quarters of the [[Ordahir Kobold Colony]].

Before they could leave, they found something worth finding.

In the prison block — where the goblin prisoners still prayed loudly to [[Durnn]] — a desk had a hidden compartment. Inside: letters. One from [[Durnn]] to [[Ragh]], confirming in writing what Ragh had admitted only in words. The cross-faction conspiracy was no longer just his word against the situation. It was ink.

A second letter was older and stranger — from [[Belak]] to [[Ragh]], cryptic in the way of men who do not commit openly to page. Belak was considering whether to bring Ragh "into the fold." Whatever that meant, it placed Ragh in Belak's orbit before the events upstairs had fully played out.

The party pocketed both.

## Into the Dark with Meepo

Morning arrived the way it always does underground — as a change in temperature rather than light.

[[Meepo]] led them out. He had been given this, and he was going to do it right.

The direct route toward goblin territory — through the northern sections of the Upper Level, past the barricades near [[Yusdrayl]]'s territory — was impassable. The ongoing war between the [[Ordahir Kobolds]] and [[Durbuluk Goblins]] had choked the passages with fortification and skirmish debris. Meepo knew another way: longer, northeast, through parts of the citadel no faction had fully claimed.

He took them.

---

## The Rat Room

The far northeastern corner of the [[Sunless Citadel]] was the kind of place that accumulates violence by geography. Traps first — the party moved carefully, reading pressure stones and trigger lines. Then the rats.

Not ordinary rats. A brood. At the back: a Huge Rat Brood-Mother, territorial and ancient, commanding her offspring with the authority of a thing that has bred death into a room for long enough that the room now belongs to her.

They killed her. The room went quiet.

Then one of them found [[Karakas]].

He was in the corner, half-eaten, his woodsman's gear torn and scattered. The man who had guided the Hucrele delegation through the marshes, through forty-plus miles of wetlands, through every obstacle that stood between Sunhold and the citadel — had made it this far, and no farther. The rats had done it. Not goblins, not traps, not whatever waited below. Rats.

The room had a crumbled wall and roof. Through the opening, light leaked in — the rift itself, the great geological wound in which the [[Sunless Citadel]] sat. The outside world, visible but unreachable.

## Left Behind

The party doubled back toward goblin territory.

Here they made the decision that had been coming since they first understood the situation: [[Meepo]] could not go with them. Taking a kobold into Durbuluk Goblin territory, at war with the kobolds, was not a plan. It was a way to get someone killed.

They left him in the rat room — cleared of brood-mothers, open to the rift. They left him food and water. They told him where they were going.

Meepo received this without complaint. He had not expected otherwise.

---

## The Ruse at the Door

The party found the side entrance to the [[Durbuluk Goblins]]' section of the Upper Level and stepped through.

Goblin guards, bows trained. A frozen moment.

[[Jonathan Jordan]] moved first — or perhaps [[Ember]], or whoever among them had the presence to make the first words count. The claim was simple: alliance with [[Ragh]] and [[Durnn]], confirmed. The letters helped. They had ink.

An older goblin came forward to hear them out. This was [[Grenl]].

She listened. She read — or had it read to her — and she made a decision that was less about belief and more about consequence: let them in, see what they wanted, keep the peace. She led them in.

---

## The Wyrmling's Terms

[[Calcryx]] was kept in a cell. The chain around her was heavy and well-fastened — except for one link, which the party discreetly pointed out would soon give way.

Calcryx was not grateful in the conventional sense. She was a dragon, even a young one, and gratitude implies vulnerability she did not intend to show. What she made clear: she had no loyalty to the [[Ordahir Kobolds]], and she was not returning to them. Yusdrayl's claim on her was political, not personal, and she knew the difference.

But [[Meepo]] was different. Meepo had cared for her — genuinely, without agenda. The party told her where to find Meepo: the rat room to the northeast, supplied.

Calcryx said she owed the party a favor. She said it like someone who tracks debts and intends to settle them.

Then, before the party left the cell block, Calcryx turned toward the door — where Durbuluk goblins had been waiting outside — and exhaled.

A thin breath of frost, contemptuous and precise. Minor damage. Maximum statement.

## Grenl's Accounting

[[Durnn]] was not present. He was elsewhere in the citadel, or elsewhere entirely. The party would need to wait.

Grenl led them to what served as the colony's food store — a pantry, cleared of threat, adequate for rest.

In the time before sleep, they learned her shape.

She had been one of the [[Durbuluk Goblins]]' elders. Then [[Durnn]] came to power, and the elders who had held power before him did not survive the transition. She alone had not opposed him — not because she agreed with him, not because she approved of the war, but because she understood that opposing him would accomplish nothing except her death.

She had chosen to live.

She disapproved of the war. She disapproved of Durnn's proactive hostility toward the kobolds, the grinding attrition of a conflict she believed served no one. She kept these opinions to herself. Keeping them to herself was how she had stayed alive.

The party rested in the pantry. Durnn might come the next day.
