## Description
Helm is the god of vigilance, protection, and watchful guardianship. He appears as an imposing, fully armored figure whose features are concealed behind a great helm — a deliberate symbol of his nature, for the Watcher sets aside personal glory in favour of unwavering duty. His presence radiates quiet strength and iron resolve, and those who call upon him feel the weight of his gaze: constant, measuring, and never blinking.

During the Time of Troubles, Helm alone stood guard at the celestial stairways, barring even fellow gods from passage. That act earned him both reverence and resentment, yet it proved the depth of his commitment. His worship has endured through dark seasons, for his faithful hold that the Watcher can never truly be vanquished — so long as one sentinel remains at their post, Helm's light persists.

## Tenets
- Be ever vigilant. A guard who sleeps is no guard at all.
- Favour patience, clear thought, and careful planning over rash action.
- Protect the innocent and defend those who cannot defend themselves.
- Duty comes before personal desire; the post must never be abandoned.
- Strength without discipline is chaos — temper power with purpose.

## Followers & Influence
Helm's faithful include bodyguards, city watch members, treasury guards, and any who have sworn an oath of protection. In [[Crystal City]], his emblem is prominently displayed upon the [[Adamantine Guild]], where it stands alongside the symbols of [[Oghma]] and [[Waukeen]] as one of the Guild's three founding pillars — representing protection, knowledge, and progress.

The [[Adamantine Guild]] currently struggles with severe understaffing and a shortage of healers following attacks by shadow creatures, making Helm's call to vigilance more urgent than ever. The Guild actively recruits capable individuals to bolster the defence of the [[Jade Spire]].

[[Augustus Kennedy]], a warforged seeker of purpose, has recognised Helm as a potential patron deity. His combat training at the [[Adamantine Guild]] under [[Luks Borg]] aligns closely with Helm's ideals of strength and unwavering protection, though Augustus also contemplates allegiance to [[Tyr]] or [[Torm]].
