## Description
Torm is the god of duty, loyalty, and righteous courage. He is depicted as a noble warrior whose bearing speaks not of conquest but of service — a sword raised in defence of the helpless, never in pursuit of personal glory. Among the triad of law-and-defence deities that includes [[Helm]] and [[Tyr]], Torm represents the personal dimension of honour: the oath kept at great cost, the comrade who never falters, the knight who places duty above survival.

His nature is deeply martial, yet tempered by compassion. Torm does not glorify war for its own sake; he endures it because the innocent cannot. Every follower who bears his symbol carries an implicit promise — that they will stand between the defenceless and whatever threatens them, no matter the odds.

## Tenets
- Duty is sacred. An oath sworn is an oath kept, whatever the cost.
- Defend the innocent and the weak; your strength exists to serve others.
- Loyalty to your comrades and your cause must never waver.
- Righteousness demands action, not merely contemplation — stand and fight when the moment calls.
- Lead by example; let your conduct inspire others to honour and service.

## Followers & Influence
Torm's faithful are paladins, clerics, knights, and soldiers who define themselves by their oaths of service. His worship is closely intertwined with that of [[Tyr]] and [[Helm]], the three deities forming an allied triad that emphasises justice, vigilance, and loyal duty.

[[Augustus Kennedy]], a warforged grappling with questions of identity and purpose, considers Torm among his potential patron deities. Augustus's combat training and growing commitment to the role of protector align naturally with Torm's teachings, and his deliberation over which of the three allied gods to follow reflects the depth with which he approaches his divine calling. His journey of self-discovery — balancing martial discipline with a search for meaning — embodies the kind of earnest, duty-bound devotion that Torm values most.
