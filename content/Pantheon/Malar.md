## Description
Malar is the god of the hunt at its most savage — the thrill of the chase, the spray of blood, the dominance of predator over prey. Where [[Mielikki]] sees the forest as something to cherish and [[Silvanus]] accepts its harsh cycles with solemn reverence, Malar revels in its brutality. He embodies the darkest impulses of the wild: nature not as sanctuary, but as arena.

His followers do not hunt out of necessity. They hunt for sport, for cruelty, for the intoxicating rush of power over a creature that cannot fight back. The Beastlord whispers that the wilderness exists to be conquered, that mercy is weakness, and that only the strong deserve to endure. This philosophy places him in direct and bitter opposition to the other nature deities of the pantheon.

## Tenets
- The hunt is sacred — pursue it with savage joy.
- Strength and dominance are the only virtues that matter.
- Mercy is the luxury of the weak; the wild does not forgive.
- Revel in the fear of prey; it is an offering to the Beastlord.
- Civilization is a cage — tear it down and let the wild reclaim what is its own.

## Followers & Influence
Malar's worshippers are feared and reviled throughout [[Corranor]]. They include sport hunters, poachers, lycanthropes, and those who take pleasure in bloodshed under the guise of honouring "the natural order." Their rituals are violent affairs, often culminating in ceremonial hunts through wild or settled lands alike.

[[Sylvara Oakwhisper]], a half-elf ranger of [[Mielikki]] and mentor to [[Ember]], names Malar's followers as one of the gravest threats to the balance of the natural world. She teaches that their creed — viewing the wild as something to conquer rather than cherish — stands as the antithesis of everything the forest's guardians protect. The tension between Malar's faithful and the followers of [[Mielikki]] and [[Silvanus]] is an enduring source of conflict in the wilds of [[Aenath]].
