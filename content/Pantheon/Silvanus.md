## Description
Silvanus is the god of wild nature, the untamed places, and the primal forces that govern the earth. He is a figure of strength and solemn serenity, his presence resonant with the ancient power of old-growth forests, windswept moors, and deep caverns where no mortal foot has trod. He does not prettify the wilderness or soften its edges — Silvanus sees nature as it truly is, beautiful and brutal in equal measure.

Where [[Mielikki]] advocates for compassion and coexistence between the wild and civilisation, Silvanus takes a harder view. Suffering, death, and decay are as essential to the cycle as growth and renewal. His druids do not weep for the deer taken by the wolf; they understand that the wolf's hunger serves the forest. This acceptance of nature's harsher truths sets Silvanus apart from his gentler counterpart, though both stand united against the depredations of [[Malar]].

## Tenets
- Nature's laws are absolute. Honour them without sentiment or hesitation.
- Preserve the wilderness in all its forms — beauty and brutality alike.
- Accept suffering and death as necessary parts of the natural cycle.
- Civilisation must not be allowed to devour the wild; hold the boundary firm.
- Understand the raw forces of the world before presuming to shape them.

## Followers & Influence
Silvanus is revered by druids, nature wardens, and those who dwell in the deep wild far from roads and cities. His followers tend to be less approachable than those of [[Mielikki]] — they are ascetics and guardians who enforce nature's balance with unflinching resolve, even when that balance demands hard choices.

[[Sylvara Oakwhisper]], a half-elf ranger of [[Mielikki]] and mentor to [[Ember]], acknowledges the philosophical divide between her faith and that of Silvanus. She respects his druids but notes that their willingness to accept suffering as intrinsic to life can make them difficult allies. The tension between the two traditions — compassionate stewardship versus impartial natural law — is one of the defining debates among the nature-faithful of [[Aenath]].
