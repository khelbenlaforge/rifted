## Description

Gond is the god of craft, invention, and the act of making. Not merely smithing or engineering — *all* creation that involves transforming raw material into something purposeful falls under his purview. He is the divine hand that shapes, tempers, and refines. Where [[Mystra]] governs the invisible laws of magic, Gond governs the tangible work of building — and in the cosmology revealed within the [[Sunless Citadel]], their roles are complementary halves of the same act.

In the Sanctum beneath the Sealed Door, Gond is depicted standing opposite [[Mystra]], a smith's hammer raised above the scale model of [[Aenath]]. Not threatening — *shaping*. The world as something still being forged, still being tempered, still becoming whatever it was meant to be. If Mystra holds the world in being, Gond is the one who gives it form.

## Tenets

- Create. Improve. Innovate. The act of making is sacred.
- No invention is inherently good or evil — only its use determines its worth.
- Share discoveries and techniques; hoarding knowledge stifles progress.
- Respect the raw materials of creation — the forge consumes, but the smith must give back.

## Followers & Influence

Smiths, engineers, tinkers, and inventors pay homage to Gond. His influence in [[Corranor]] is less visible than that of [[Mystra]] or [[Oghma]], but no less foundational — every tool forged, every building raised, every mechanism assembled carries his blessing whether acknowledged or not. The frontier settlement of [[Sunhold]], built by miners and prospectors from raw timber and canvas, is Gond's domain in practice if not in name.

## Connections

- **[[Mystra]]** — Paired with her in the Sanctum statuary. Gond shapes what Mystra sustains — their partnership in the Sanctum suggests a cosmological relationship at the very foundation of [[Aenath]].
- **[[Bahamut]] and [[Takhisis]]** — Flanking the Mystra-Gond arrangement as protectors of the world they craft and sustain.
