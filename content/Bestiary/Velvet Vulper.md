## Description

A fox-like fey creature with fur that shimmers in hues of silver and violet, shifting like silk under moonlight. Its eyes gleam with mischievous intelligence, and its bushy tail leaves faint trails of luminescent dust when it moves. Velvet Vulpers are collectors and tricksters — their burrows glitter with stolen trinkets, taken not for greed but for beauty.

## Ecology & Behavior

**Habitat:** Moonlit glens, fey-touched forests, and the edges of enchanted groves. They are most active at dusk and dawn, sleeping through the brightest hours.

**Diet:** Omnivorous, with a preference for berries, moths, and small fey insects. They can be befriended with offerings of silver coins or moonwater.

**Temperament:** Chaotic but loyal once bonded. A Velvet Vulper will steal from strangers without hesitation but fiercely protects those it considers part of its "den." Their fur shifts color to express mood — bright silver when excited, deep violet when wary.

## Role in the World

Fey courts sometimes employ Velvet Vulpers as messengers and scouts, trusting their speed and natural invisibility. Among mortals, they are regarded as omens of the fey — seeing one near a settlement usually means the boundary between worlds is thinning. Rangers and rogues prize them as companions for their stealth and charm abilities.

### As a Companion

The Velvet Vulper forms an intuitive empathic link with its master, synchronizing stealth and ambush tactics. When your Ranger takes the Hide action, the Vulper may Hide as a bonus action. At higher levels, its Illusory Flicker can be used twice per short rest to mirror Beast Master improvements.

- A noble's heirloom has been stolen, but the thief's footprints end at a grove where dancing lights flicker between the trees.
- A Velvet Vulper has taken up residence in a tavern's attic, charming patrons into leaving their coin purses unattended. The innkeeper wants it gone — but the regulars have grown fond of it.
