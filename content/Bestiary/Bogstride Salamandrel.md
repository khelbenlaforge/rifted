## Description

A sleek, amphibious creature roughly the size of a Clydesdale horse, the Bogstride Salamandrel's skin gleams with slick, dark-green luminescence that shifts like sunlight through algae. It has four broad, splayed legs ending in webbed, claw-tipped toes; a wide head with frilled gills; and an expressive face that always seems faintly bemused. Along its flanks, thin bioluminescent lines pulse with each breath, echoing the flow of water through its body.

Its belly sports a translucent membrane that vibrates rapidly, allowing it to "hydroplane" across mud and swampy ground at surprising speed — a sound somewhere between a purr and the murmur of a bubbling stream.

## Ecology & Behavior

**Habitat:** Bogstride Salamandrels dwell in deep marshlands, fen forests, and mist-covered deltas where most mounts would sink or drown. They construct burrow-nests in half-submerged logs and are fiercely territorial toward large predators like crocodiles or giant frogs.

**Diet:** Omnivorous, though primarily insectivorous. They particularly enjoy fireflies, which give their skin an extra shimmer. They have also been known to steal shiny coins or gems, confusing them for beetles.

**Temperament:** Intelligent and empathic. Bogstride Salamandrels form strong emotional bonds with riders and display moods through color pulses:

- Soft green — calm/content
- Pale blue — anxious or alert
- Yellow — curious
- Crimson — threatened or angry

When frightened, they can stiffen their entire body, secreting a resin that hardens into a stone-like shell for several hours. While petrified, they are immune to most damage but cannot move.

## Role in the World

**Swamp Folk Mounts:** Used by marsh-dwelling rangers, druids, and witch-folk as both transport and guardian. Their ability to skim across bogs makes them indispensable for traversing treacherous wetlands.

**Religious Symbol:** Some Druidic circles revere the Salamandrel as a symbol of patience and renewal — an embodiment of nature's ability to turn decay into life. Its petrification defense is sometimes viewed as an act of "becoming stone to remember the stillness of the earth."

**Companion Quirk:** They hum rhythmically while resting, the tone matching the local water current. Clever riders learn to listen to these hums as an early warning of disturbances or unnatural magic.

## D&D 5e Stat Block



### Mount Rules

A trained Bogstride Salamandrel accepts a single rider and can carry up to 600 lbs. It requires daily immersion in water for at least one hour or it becomes sluggish (speed halved until it rests).

When used as a combat mount, the Salamandrel can take the _Dodge_, _Dash_, or _Disengage_ action independently, showing its semi-sentient awareness of battlefield danger.

### Tack: Amphibious Saddle System ("Bogseat")

**Design:** A flexible saddle harnessed with oil-treated leather and reinforced reed-weave, made to float independently for short periods. Its seat is low and slightly reclined, allowing the rider's legs to dangle close to the Salamandrel's sides for better balance during aquatic travel. The saddle features detachable foot-loops that double as swim fins.

**Mechanical Benefit:**
- Grants advantage on Dexterity saving throws to avoid falling prone when the mount submerges or surfaces suddenly.
- The saddle stays buoyant if dislodged, keeping the rider afloat automatically.

**Crafting Difficulty:** DC 15 Leatherworker's Tools or Weaver's Tools.
**Market Price:** 250 gp.
