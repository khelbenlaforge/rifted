## Description

A small, ember-hearted creature that moves in bounding leaps, trailing sparks like a living firework. Its hide is rough and ash-gray, cracked through with veins of molten orange that pulse with each hop. Groups of Cinderhops — called _sparks_ — leave trails of flickering light when startled, turning a volcanic hillside into a cascade of fireflies.

## Ecology & Behavior

**Habitat:** Volcanic slopes, geothermal vents, and planar rifts where the Elemental Plane of Fire bleeds through. They nest in warm ash piles, glowing faintly from within while sleeping.

**Diet:** Minerals and embers. They feed on ignite deposits and cooled magma, extracting trace metals through their skin.

**Temperament:** Curious and energetic. Cinderhops are not aggressive but their heated bodies make casual handling dangerous. They can be tamed with patience and fire-resistant gloves.

## Role in the World

Cinderhops are prized as companions by rangers and druids who operate in volcanic or arid regions. Their innate warmth makes them invaluable in cold climates, and their fire resistance allows them to scout lava flows and geothermal caves. Smiths sometimes keep them around forges as living bellows-warmers — though this occasionally goes wrong.

### As a Companion

When bonded to a Ranger or Druid, the Cinderhop's fiery presence lends warmth and morale. It gains a limited empathic bond with its master, conveying emotions through sparks and tail flicks. At your DM's discretion, the Cinderhop's Fiery Leap can scale to 1d8 → 2d8 fire damage at level 8+ to match Beast Companion progression.

## Adventure Hooks
- A town's forges are mysteriously overheating — turns out a family of Cinderhops nested in the bellows.
- A spark of Cinderhops has been driven from their volcanic home by something deeper in the caldera. They're now causing brush fires in nearby farmland.
