## Description

A sinuous plant creature grown from the seeds of the [[Goblin Fruit|Gulthias fruit]] — or something older that the tree learned to produce. It moves like a snake but is made entirely of dense, dark vine matter, its body articulated like a whip. When still, it is nearly indistinguishable from a tangled root cluster. When it strikes, it latches and holds, and whatever it has latched onto finds itself unable to move. Its bite carries a necromantic chill, the same energy that suffuses everything [[Belak]] cultivates.

The party encountered it as a "vine serpent" in [[Durnn's Research Lab]] during Session 12, where it emerged from plant growth alongside a twig blight swarm. Its true name — Blightsnake — comes from [[Belak]]'s grove taxonomy.

## Ecology & Behavior

**Origin:** A creature of [[Belak]]'s making, grown from the corruption of the [[Gulthias Tree]]. Like the twig blights, it is a child of the tree — distinct in form but sharing the same necromantic/draconic magical signature as the [[Goblin Fruit]].

**Hunting:** Ambush predator. It remains motionless among roots and vine matter until prey is within lunge range, then strikes and latches. Once attached, it paralyzes its target and feeds at leisure.

**Paralytic grip:** The latch is the danger. Once the Blightsnake bites and latches, both it and the target become paralyzed — requiring an ally to physically tear it free (DC 15 Strength check).

**Languages:** Speaks Common and Goblin — useful for receiving orders from goblin handlers, suggesting [[Belak]] has integrated them into his operation as managed creatures rather than feral ones.

## Role in the World

A lieutenant-tier creature in Belak's grove ecosystem — more dangerous than a twig blight, less visible than the plant lieutenants with bark-like skin. Likely deployed to guard specific locations or subdue targets Belak wants taken alive.
